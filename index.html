Introduction

Christians worship one God in three persons--the Father, the Son (i.e., Jesus) and the Holy Spirit.

They do so because God has clearly revealed the doctrine of the Holy Trinity in the inspired Scriptures. They don't accept the doctrine of the Trinity just because it "feels right" to them or because of a decree from some pope or church council. Rather, they believe and cherish the truth of who God is because God has made it known in the Bible.

The doctrine is summed up in the statement that God is one being in three fully-divine persons. This definition is a summary of seven fundamental tenets, each clearly revealed in the Scriptures:

  • There is but one God.
  • The Father is God.
  • The Son is God.
  • The Holy Spirit is God.
  • The Father is not the Son.
  • The Son is not the Holy Spirit.
  • The Holy Spirit is not the Father.

To reject any one of these basic truths is to reject God and His self-revelation.

Christians have always condemned the rejection of any one of these seven biblical points as damnable heresy. If you reject the first tenet (i.e., Monotheism: "There is but one God"), you end up embracing atheism or a form of polytheism. The rejection of any of points 2-4 (i.e., Co-equality of the persons: "The Father is God," "The Son is God," "The Holy Spirit is God") leads to some variation of the ancient heresy of Arianism, which is the belief that Jesus and the Holy Spirit are not fully equal to God the Father. Finally, to dismiss any one of the last three points (i.e., Distinction of the persons: "The Father is not the Son," "The Son is not the Holy Spirit," "The Holy Spirit is not the Father") is to identify with some form of the ancient heresy of modalism.

This page presents some of the key biblical texts that most clearly illustrate these essential truths that undergird the doctrine of the Holy Trinity.

The doctrine of the Trinity goes beyond human reason--but is not against reason.

Christians do not believe that 1 + 1 + 1 = 1. Rather, they acknowledge one divine being in three persons: one what in three who's. The categorical distinction between being and personhood is one that you use every day: a pebble has being but lacks personhood; but humans have both being and personhood.

The Bible is not a textbook of Systematic Theology.

God revealed His Tri-unity during the inter-testamental period of history: specifically, in the Incarnation and at Pentecost. Therefore, the New Testament authors did not lay out the doctrine of the Trinity in a step-by-step manner such as the seven-point outline here. Instead, they conveyed their recognition of God's Tri-unity as it related to their specific narrative or didactic purposes.

The following texts illustrate some of the ways that the biblical authors included the seven basic points of Trinitarian doctrine in their writings.

Within the one Being that is God, there exist eternally three co-equal and co-eternal persons, namely, the Father, the Son and the Holy Spirit.
White, James. The Forgotten Trinity. Minneapolis, MN: Bethany House Publishers, 1998. 26.

There is but one God.

  • Deuteronomy 6:4
    Yahweh is one being.
  • Isaiah 43:10
    Yahweh is the first and the last. There is no other god besides Him.
  • Isaiah 44:6, 8
    Yahweh knows of no other god.
“Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God, the LORD is one."
Deuteronomy 6:4, ESV

The Father is God.

  • John 11:41-42
    Jesus prayed to the Father.
  • John 17:3
    Jesus identifies the Father as the one true God.
  • Romans 1:7
    Paul identifies the Father as God.
Grace to you and peace from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.
II Corinthians 1:3, ESV

The Son is God.

  • John 1:1
    The Son has always existed from eternity past. The Son has always been fully divine in nature (cf. Edward Dalcour on John 1:1).
  • John 1:3
    The Son created all that has come into being (cf. Isaiah 44:24).
  • John 20:28
    Thomas refers to Jesus as "the Lord of me and the God of me" (cf. Psalm 35:24).
  • Romans 9:5; Titus 2:13
    Paul refers to Jesus as God.
  • I John 5:20
    John refers to Jesus as "the true God."
  • Revelation 5:13-14
    Every creature in heaven and on earth and under the earth and in the sea, and all that is in them worship both the Son and the Father in heaven.
In [Jesus] the whole fullness of deity dwells bodily.
Colossians 2:9, ESV

The Holy Spirit is God.

  • Matthew 28:19
    The Holy Spirit shares the one name of God together with the Father and the Son.
  • Acts 5:1-4
    Peter refers to the Holy Spirit as God.
  • Acts 28:25-27
    Paul quotes the Holy Spirit, referencing him as Yahweh.
The grace of the Lord Jesus Christ and the love of God and the fellowship of the Holy Spirit be with you all.
I Corinthians 13:14, ESV

The Father is not the Son.

  • John 1:1
    The Father was with the Son from eternity past (cf. I John 1:1-2).
  • John 17:1-5
    The Father and the Son glorify each other.
  • Mark 15:34
    At Jesus' crucifixion, the Father related to the Son as a judge relates to the one judged.
  • Colossians 1:13
    The Father loves the Son.
  • John 8:14-18
    The Father and the Son are two distinct witnesses.
  • John 6:32-40
    The Father sent the Son.
  • II John 3, 9
    The Father and the Son are distinct.
Now when all the people were baptized, and when Jesus also had been baptized and was praying, the heavens were opened, and the Holy Spirit descended on him in bodily form, like a dove; and a voice came from heaven, “You are my beloved Son; with you I am well pleased.”
Luke 3:21-22

The Son is not the Holy Spirit.

  • Matthew 12:28
    The Son, Jesus, drove out demons "by the Spirit of God."
  • Matthew 12:31-32
    Blasphemy against the Holy Spirit is distinct from blasphemy against the Son.
  • John 15:26
    The Holy Spirit testifies about the Son.
  • John 16:7-11
    Jesus sends the Holy Spirit.
  • John 16:14
    The Holy Spirit glorifies the Son.
"I will ask the Father, and he will give you another Helper, to be with you forever, even the Spirit of truth..."
Jesus, John 14:16-17

The Holy Spirit is not the Father.

  • John 14:17
    The Holy Spirit is a distinct person from the Son and the Father.
  • Luke 11:13
    The Father gives the Holy Spirit (cf. John 14:16).
  • John 14:26
    The Father sends the Holy Spirit.
I do not cease to give thanks for you, remembering you in my prayers, that the God of our Lord Jesus Christ, the Father of glory, may give you the Spirit of wisdom and of revelation in the knowledge of him.
Ephesians 1:16-17
